I'm currently based in Accra, Ghana, while making plans to relocate to the United Kingdom in search of better opportunities — not only to earn more, but to grow more deeply as a software engineer.

This is a transitional season for me. I have moved on from my previous job, and I'm using this time intentionally: to grow as a father, to take on more responsibility at home, to build, to learn, and to become sharper in the areas that matter to me.

## Work

Professionally, I'm currently between roles and open to the right opportunity.

I'm especially interested in work that helps me stretch as a software engineer: technically challenging products, thoughtful teams, strong engineering culture, and environments where I can continue growing in mobile engineering, product thinking, and software architecture.

## Building

Right now, I'm working on **Celia** — a time-blocking and discipline app named after my daughter.

The idea is personal: helping people take back their time from distraction and spend it on what actually matters. It sits somewhere between focus, self-discipline, accountability, and care.

I'm still shaping the product, but the heart of it is simple: build a calmer, more intentional way to interrupt doomscrolling and make room for better choices.

## Learning

Technically, I'm currently honing my skills in native mobile development:

- Android, using Kotlin
- iOS, using Swift

My focus is not just on learning syntax or frameworks, but on becoming better at building well-structured, maintainable mobile applications.

Creatively, I'm also taking more street photographs. Photography gives me a different way to observe the world, slow down, and notice ordinary moments with more care.

## Health

I'm also paying closer attention to my health.

With some time now available, I've dedicated a couple of hours a day, four days a week, to working out. The goal is not to chase an extreme physique, but to stay lean, healthy, and strong enough to show up well for my life.

## Reading

I maintain that I'm not an avid reader. I'm not the kind of guy that boasts about reading 100 books a year. I plan on reading **one** book each month, starting with [**Inside the Box: How Constraints Make Us Better by David Epstein**](https://a.co/d/05Cn54oB). It feels like the right kind of book for this season: a reminder that limits do not always hold us back. Sometimes, they give us the structure we need to think better, build better, and become more focused.

## What I'm avoiding

I'm trying to say no to overthinking and distractions.

Both can quietly take more from me than I realise: focus, confidence, time, energy, and momentum. This season is about being more deliberate with what gets my attention.

## Home

_I'm keeping this part private._

What matters publicly is this: family is a big part of this season, and a lot of the work I'm doing right now is connected to becoming more present, useful, and dependable at home.

## Current Mood

Calm. Honest. Minimal. Reflective. Technical.

I'm in a season of rebuilding and becoming — not loudly, but intentionally.
